Measuring Performance: The EEMUA-191 Benchmark

Effective alarm management isn't subjective. International standards provide clear KPIs to measure the health of an alarm system from the operator's perspective. The goal is to move from an "Unacceptable" state to an "Acceptable" one.

The Solution: The Alarm Philosophy Document (APD)

As mandated by ISA-18.2, the APD is the cornerstone of governance. It's the formal, site-specific standard that defines every rule for the alarm system, ensuring consistency and preventing degradation.

Roles & Responsibilities

Defines ownership for rationalization, MOC, and monitoring to ensure accountability.

Prioritization Method

Establishes the objective process (e.g., risk matrix) for assigning priorities to aid operator discrimination.

Alarm Design Principles

Sets rules for deadbands, delays, and suppression to prevent nuisance alarms at the source.

Management of Change (MOC)

Defines the formal procedure for all permanent changes to maintain the integrity of the rationalized system.

Strategy: Effective Prioritization

The goal of prioritization is **discrimination**. During an upset, operators must instantly identify what matters most. A healthy system follows a strict distribution, making high-priority alarms rare and therefore significant.

Process: Introduction to Rationalization

Rationalization is the systematic process of reviewing every potential alarm to ensure it is a help, not a hindrance. This creates the **Master Alarm Database (MAD)**, the single source of truth for all alarms.
